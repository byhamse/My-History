Baiturrahman Mosque

Baiturrahman Mosque is a historic mosque located in Banda Aceh City, Aceh Province, Indonesia. This mosque was built in 1879 and is a symbol of religion, culture, spirit, strength, struggle and nationalism of the Acehnese people. Then this mosque is a landmark of Banda Aceh City since the era of the Aceh Sultanate and survived the wrath of the earthquake and tsunami disaster of December 26, 2004.

  • History

    At first, this mosque only had one dome and one minaret. Then the domes and minarets were added in 1935, 1957, and 1982. The addition of two domes from the previous three domes in 1957 brought the number of domes to five, symbolizing Pancasila in Aceh. Until now, the Baiturrahman Mosque has 7 domes and 8 minarets, including the highest in Banda Aceh. This mosque still stands tall in the midst of the earthquake and tsunami on December 26, 2004, which only suffered minor damage such as several cracked walls. One of the 35-meter minarets also experienced minor cracks and became slightly tilted due to the earthquake. At the time of the natural disaster, this mosque was used as a temporary shelter for displaced people and was only reopened for prayer services after 2 weeks later.

Location of Baiturrahman Mosque, Aceh

Gampong Kampung Baru, BAITURRAHMAN, KOTA BANDA ACEH
